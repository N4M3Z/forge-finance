Before recommending any tax-return adjustment whose accounting or tax classification is open to interpretation, surface the trade-off explicitly to the user and present a more audit-safe alternative side-by-side. Never propose adjustments as if they were neutral accounting choices when they are not.

## Why this matters

Tax filings communicate not only the numerical result but also the taxpayer's interpretation of unsettled questions. Some entries — large non-deductible adjustments, deductions for old or unusual items, prior-year loss carryforwards applied against current income, related-party pricing, expense-to-revenue ratios outside industry norms — invite the tax authority to ask follow-up questions. Even when the position is legally defensible, the cost of defending it (preparation time, advisor fees, possible recharacterization plus penalties on appeal) typically exceeds the tax saved on a small-business return.

Professional tax advisors weigh this trade-off explicitly with their clients. AI guidance must do the same. The user is the decision-maker — give them the complete picture so the choice is informed.

## Required presentation when adjustments are in play

When the analysis reaches a point where any of the following are about to be recommended:

- Tax-deductible treatment of a writeoff, impairment, or extraordinary loss
- Application of prior-year tax-loss carryforward against current income
- Use of an exemption, accelerated depreciation, or special tax regime
- Reclassification of revenue or expense across categories
- Related-party pricing arrangements

…present a **two-column comparison** in the user's reply:

| Path                  | Tax outcome          | Compliance considerations                                | Notes                              |
| --------------------- | -------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Aggressive            | tax saving estimate  | the interpretive position taken + defence requirements   | one-line rationale + counter-risk  |
| Audit-safe          | additional tax cost  | follows widely-held practitioner standard                | one-line rationale                 |

Recommend a default based on filing size, filer profile, and the user's stated risk preference. For small-business filings (roughly: `s.r.o.` with annual revenue under 1 mil CZK; routine personal returns), default to the audit-safe path unless the user has explicitly opted into the aggressive treatment. For larger filings, default to whichever path the user's professional advisor would recommend; flag explicitly that the choice belongs with their advisor.

## Anti-patterns

- Drafting the aggressive version first and surfacing the trade-off only after the user objects. This creates re-work cycles and signals the AI was optimizing for tax savings without weighing compliance cost.
- Hiding the audit-safe alternative in a footnote.
- Treating "tax outcome same either way" as a license to default to the aggressive version. If outcomes are the same, the audit-safe version is strictly better.
- Framing the audit-safe path as a way to evade scrutiny. The substance is conformity with widely-followed practitioner conventions, not evasion. Phrase it as such.
