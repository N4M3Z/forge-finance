Non-English legal and financial terms appear in backticks: `bytové potřeby`, `daňové potvrzení k DIP`. English equivalents go in plain prose. Use the international name (LTIP, supplementary pension savings) in running text; Czech abbreviations (`DIP`, `DPFO`) only inside backtick context.

Do not write czenglish — sentences mixing un-backticked Czech words with English. If a Czech term is needed inline, either translate it to English or wrap it in backticks. Never split: write either "the contract was duly filed" or "the contract was duly `přihlášena`", never "the contract was duly přihlášena".
